Career & Education

Dr. Prabhu C. Ganiger is a senior entomologist specializing in Diptera taxonomy and ecology. Over his 25-year career, he has led field surveys, curated museum collections, and published extensively on fruit flies, leaf miners, and nuisance Diptera.

His research focuses on integrative approaches combining morphological, molecular, and ecological data to understand dipteran biodiversity, particularly in vulnerable ecosystems.

"To build a global Diptera research community—sharing taxonomy, data, and discoveries."

Global Survey of Bactrocera

This comprehensive study is documenting the diversity, distribution, and host associations of Bactrocera fruit flies across tropical Asia and the Pacific.

Field expeditions have collected specimens at over 120 sites in 8 countries, revealing patterns of species radiation and ecological adaptation.

Collections & Diptera

Our Collections

The Ganiger Lab maintains one of South Asia's most comprehensive Diptera collections, with over 250,000 curated specimens representing 75 families and more than 3,500 species.

Following the Smithsonian model, our collection preserves biodiversity data and serves as a vital resource for taxonomic research and ecosystem monitoring.

Why Your Support Matters

Diptera research is fundamental to understanding ecosystem health, agricultural productivity, and disease vector control. Despite their ecological importance, dipterans remain understudied in many regions of the world.

Your donations help fund critical research components including field expeditions to biodiversity hotspots, molecular analysis tools, and student scholarships that train the next generation of entomologists.

With your support, we can expand our research on fly systematics, ecology, and management to address pressing environmental challenges.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where does my gift go?

85% of donations directly fund research activities including field work, laboratory supplies, and student support. The remaining 15% supports public outreach programs and open-access publication fees.

How can I request funding?

Graduate students can apply for our annual research grants each January. Download the application guidelines from our Resources section.

Is my donation tax-deductible?

Yes, all donations to the Ganiger Diptera Lab through UAS Bangalore are eligible for tax exemptions under Section 80G of the Indian Income Tax Act.
